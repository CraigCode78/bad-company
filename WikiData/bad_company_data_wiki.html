KnowTheChain
knowthechain.org
Benchmarks major companies on forced labour risk across global supply chains — scored 0–100
Free Tier 1 ▾

What it covers

Annual benchmarks of 45–60 of the world's largest companies in apparel and footwear, food and beverage, and ICT. Scores companies 0–100 across seven themes: commitment and governance, traceability, risk assessment, purchasing practices, recruitment, worker voice, and remedy. Publicly available company scorecards with downloadable datasets.

How to use in Bad Company

  • Direct score input for the Labour dimension
  • Identifies specific supply chain red flags (e.g. Xinjiang/Uyghur forced labour)
  • Benchmark trajectory data to reward improvement
  • Use as a Tier 1 anchor — cite in methodology documentation

Access & URL

knowthechain.org

Free public access to benchmark reports, company scorecards, and downloadable datasets. No API currently — scrape or download Excel files.

Notes & limitations

Covers three sectors only. Updated annually. Average ICT company scored just 20/100 in 2025; food and beverage average was 16/100. Strong on forced labour specifically, less comprehensive on wider labour conditions.

The world's most comprehensive tracker of corporate human rights allegations — 20,000+ companies, open API

  • Lawsuit database feeds the "Police Report" feature
  • Partner source to KnowTheChain data

Access & URL

Notes & limitations

World Benchmarking Alliance — Social & CHRB
worldbenchmarkingalliance.org
Social benchmark of the world's 2,000 most influential companies — 18 core social indicators, free Excel download
Free Tier 1 ▾

What it covers

The WBA Social Benchmark assesses the world's 2,000 most influential companies (the SDG2000) across 18 core social indicators spanning human rights, decent work, and ethical conduct. The Corporate Human Rights Benchmark (CHRB) goes deeper on ~100 companies across five high-risk sectors. All data is publicly downloadable in Excel format. Updated annually.

How to use in Bad Company

  • One of the broadest company universes available (2,000 companies)
  • Download the full dataset for integration into the scoring engine
  • CHRB scores feed the Labour and Governance dimensions
  • Gender Benchmark data available for gender-equity scoring

Access & URL

worldbenchmarkingalliance.org/company-scoreboard

2024 Social Benchmark data download

All benchmark data is free, open and downloadable. No API — use the Excel download links on each benchmark page.

Notes & limitations

Only 11% of assessed companies have a lobbying transparency policy. Scores are updated annually. Strongest for large public companies; coverage thins for smaller or private companies. Data is derived from public disclosures, so self-reporting bias applies.

CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project)
cdp.net
Global disclosure system for environmental data — 25,000+ companies scored A–D on climate, water, and deforestation
Partial Tier 1 ▾

What it covers

Company-reported environmental data across climate change, water security, and deforestation. Over 23,100 organisations disclosed in 2025. Scores run D– to A; A-list represents environmental leadership. Backed by 800+ institutional investors with $100 trillion in assets. Covers two-thirds of global market capitalisation. Annual cycle.

How to use in Bad Company

  • A–D score feeds directly into the Environmental dimension
  • A-list membership is a strong positive signal — rewards improvement
  • Absence of disclosure is itself a negative signal
  • Deforestation and water data goes beyond basic carbon metrics

Access & URL

cdp.net/en/data/scores

Public A-lists and headline scores are free. Full disclosure responses now require a data licence or membership. Consider a research/non-commercial agreement with CDP — they have a specific pathway for NGO/accountability use.

Notes & limitations

Data is self-reported; companies must opt in. Non-disclosure receives a score but companies can choose not to engage. Detailed response data now behind paywall as of 2025 — a significant change. Public scores remain available. Negotiate a data partnership early.

InfluenceMap / LobbyMap
influencemap.org / lobbymap.org
The world's leading database on corporate climate lobbying — 1,000 companies graded weekly, open source
Free Tier 1 ▾

What it covers

LobbyMap tracks and scores climate policy engagement for 1,000 companies and 330 industry associations globally. Scores range from A (supports Paris-aligned policy) to F (obstructing climate action). Updated weekly. Open source and free to use. Also runs FinanceMap (asset manager climate analysis) and Carbon Majors (historical emissions from 122 producers).

How to use in Bad Company

  • Unique signal: captures what companies actually lobby for, not just what they claim
  • Use LobbyMap grade as a "greenwashing risk" indicator
  • Cross-reference with CDP score — misalignment is a strong red flag
  • Feeds the "Buy Less Shit / Bad Government" pillars

Access & URL

lobbymap.org

influencemap.org/terms (open source licence)

Content is open source and free to use under InfluenceMap's terms. Data is updated weekly. No formal API, but company profiles are URL-structured and parseable.

Notes & limitations

Does not score internal company operations — focuses purely on policy engagement. Best used as a governance-plus-environment signal rather than an emissions data source. Methodology is transparent and well-documented.

Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi)
sciencebasedtargets.org
Verifies whether company net-zero targets are scientifically credible — 10,000+ companies on the Target Dashboard

What it covers

The SBTi verifies corporate net-zero and near-term targets against 1.5°C-aligned science. Target Dashboard lists all companies with validated targets or commitments, updated weekly.   • Cross-reference with CDP score to detect greenwashing claims
  • Downloadable dataset available weekly from the Target Dashboard

Access & URL

Full Target Dashboard is free and downloadable in Excel. Updated every Thursday. Registration not required for data access.

Notes & limitations

Validates targets only — not emissions performance. A company can have a verified target but still emit heavily. Use as one signal among several. Corporate Net-Zero Standard v2.0 currently in development; methodology may shift.

ftc.gov/enforcement/cases-proceedings
Official US Federal Trade Commission database of deceptive practices, dark patterns, and consumer protection cases
Free Tier 1 ▾

What it covers

The FTC's case database includes all enforcement actions for deceptive advertising, dark patterns, subscription traps, fake reviews, privacy violations, and anti-competitive behaviour. Cases include fines, consent decrees, and bans. Searchable by company name. Goes back decades with ongoing updates. Increasingly focused on digital dark patterns (2022+).

How to use in Bad Company

  • Primary Tier 1 anchor for the Consumer Manipulation dimension
  • Each enforcement action = a scored negative event with a monetary weight
  • FTC complaints database also captures consumer-reported issues
  • Directly relevant to the "Don't Get Scammed" feature pillar

Access & URL

ftc.gov/enforcement/cases-proceedings

Free public access. Structured data available. Good Jobs First's Violation Tracker already aggregates FTC data — consider using Violation Tracker as a single API rather than querying the FTC directly.

Notes & limitations

US-only. Some cases take years to resolve. Captures cases brought by the FTC, not self-reported violations. The FTC has significantly expanded dark pattern enforcement since 2022, making this increasingly relevant for digital businesses.
